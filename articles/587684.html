Best Settings for Smooth Gameplay in Cemu v2.1 on Ryzen 5 3600

If you're running Cemu v2.1 on a Ryzen 5 3600 and looking for optimal settings to achieve smooth gameplay, this guide will help you configure your emulator for the best performance. We'll focus on balancing visuals and speed, ensuring that you get the best experience possible with minimal stuttering and lag.

Graphics Settings

The following graphics settings are recommended to achieve smooth performance, especially when running demanding Wii U titles:

  • Graphics API: Vulkan
  • Graphics Device: Select your native graphics device. For the AMD Radeon(TM) Vega 8 Graphics, this is typically the integrated GPU.
  • VSync: Triple Buffering
    • Using Triple Buffering helps to smooth out gameplay and menus. Unlike Double Buffering, which may cause slow gameplay even if the menus run fine, Triple Buffering allows the GPU to work more efficiently by reducing the time spent waiting between frames. This results in a smoother overall experience without the drawbacks of screen tearing or excessive input lag.
  • Upscale Filter: Bilinear
  • Downscale Filter: Bilinear
  • Fullscreen Scaling: Keep aspect ratio

Shader Compilation

Shader compilation is essential for smooth gameplay, as it prevents stuttering when new graphical effects are rendered. Configure your shader settings as follows:

  • Asynchronous Shader Compilation: Enabled
    • Enabling this option allows shaders to compile in the background, reducing stuttering during gameplay. It's crucial for maintaining smooth performance, especially in graphically intensive games.
  • Precompiled Shader Cache: Enabled
    • Using a precompiled shader cache helps minimize the initial stuttering that can occur when running a game for the first time.

Performance Testing

With these settings on a Ryzen 5 3600 and AMD Radeon(TM) Vega 8 Graphics, my average frame rate has been around 60 frames per second, with the lowest being 54.60 fps and the highest reaching 60.50 fps. This indicates that the settings provide a stable and smooth gameplay experience for the tested games.

General Tips

Here are a few additional tips to ensure smooth performance on your Ryzen 5 3600:

  • Keep Your Drivers Updated: Make sure your graphics drivers are up to date. New drivers can improve performance and compatibility with Vulkan.
  • Close Background Applications: Free up system resources by closing unnecessary applications running in the background.
  • Use a High-Performance Power Plan: Set your power plan to High Performance in Windows to prevent CPU and GPU throttling.

Important Notes:

  • Test Settings Per Game: While these settings should provide a solid baseline, some games may require individual tweaks. Always test and adjust settings based on the specific demands of the game you're playing.
  • Graphic Mods: Some games may have graphic mods available that can further improve framerate or visual quality. Consider exploring these mods to enhance your gaming experience.
  • Tested Games: The graphics settings provided in this guide were tested on New Super Mario Bros. U and New Super Luigi U. Performance may vary with other titles.
  • Minor Graphical Glitches: Minor graphical glitches may occur, such as backgrounds changing quickly when entering underground areas or vines loading in a few frames late. These issues are typically seen when first loading certain textures or after clearing the shader cache. However, they do not impact game mechanics or overall gameplay.
  • Backup Your Configurations: Before making major changes, consider backing up your Cemu settings to revert if necessary.

This guide should help you achieve a smooth gaming experience in Cemu v2.1 on your Ryzen 5 3600. If you encounter any issues or have further questions, don't hesitate to seek advice from the Cemu community or revisit your settings for further optimization.
